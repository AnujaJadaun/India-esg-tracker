Disclosure & Reporting ● Full Scale — FY2026–27
BRSR — Business Responsibility & Sustainability Reporting
SEBI · Top 1000 listed companies for BRSR · BRSR Core assurance or assessment now mandatory for top 1000 from FY2026–27 (this financial year)
+
Applies To
BRSR mandatory: Top 1000 listed companies from FY2022–23. BRSR Core assurance or assessment: Top 1000 companies mandatory from FY2026–27 — the current financial year (April 2026 onwards). Value chain disclosures also mandatory from this FY.
Key Deadline
Annual report within 60 days of AGM. BRSR Core assurance or assessment: mandatory for top 1000 from FY2026–27 (current financial year, April 2026 – March 2027). Value chain disclosures mandatory from same FY.
Data Readiness Gap
Overall
High
So What

FY2026–27 (April 2026 – March 2027) is the year BRSR Core reaches full scale — all top 1000 listed companies must now obtain assurance or assessment on BRSR Core disclosures. Simultaneously, value chain disclosures become mandatory this financial year. A late 2024 SEBI amendment also replaced the term "reasonable assurance" with "assurance or assessment" — giving companies flexibility to choose between formal assurance or a structured assessment approach. Companies reporting for this FY are operating under the most demanding version of the framework yet.

What It Requires In Practice
  • Disclosing performance across 9 ESG principles — environment, labour, governance, community, value chain
  • BRSR Core: assured disclosure on 49 KPIs including GHG emissions, water intensity, waste, gender diversity
  • Value chain disclosure covering top suppliers and customers representing 75% of purchases and sales
  • Board sign-off and clear internal governance structures for sustainability data
  • Data collection systems with clear business unit ownership at plant level — consolidated HQ numbers will not pass assurance
Where Companies Struggle
  • Value chain and supplier data — third-party manufacturing data is inconsistent and hard to defend under assurance
  • Governance documentation — sustainability committees exist on paper but accountability trails are weak
  • Water and waste data at plant level — aggregation methodology is often inconsistent across sites
What Good Looks Like

HUL and Tata Steel run BRSR Core like a statutory audit — centralized data platforms, plant-level ownership, monthly validation cycles, pre-assurance internal reviews. Year-round process, not year-end scramble.

Practitioner's Take

FY2026–27 is the full-scale year — top 1000 mandatory, value chain disclosures mandatory, and the assurance-or-assessment terminology giving companies one last flexibility lever before full accountability sets in. The value chain piece is where most companies are currently underprepared. Data infrastructure at supplier level simply does not exist in most Indian supply chains. Companies that have not started building it are now in breach territory, not just behind schedule.

Official Sources SEBI BRSR Core Circular (Jul 2023) SEBI BRSR Original Circular (May 2021) BRSR Reporting Format (Annexure II)
02
Climate Risk & Finance ◐ Draft → 2025–26
RBI Climate Risk Disclosure Framework
Reserve Bank of India · Banks & NBFCs · Phased from FY2025–26
+
Applies To
Scheduled Commercial Banks, Urban Cooperative Banks, NBFCs above asset threshold
Key Deadline
Phased by asset size. Large banks first, smaller NBFCs over 3 years.
Data Readiness Gap
Overall
Critical
So What

For the first time, Indian banks must quantify how climate change affects their loan books and portfolios. This is a risk management mandate, not a sustainability exercise — and it creates significant demand for ESG professionals inside banks in risk, treasury, and credit functions.

Where Companies Struggle
  • Borrower-level climate risk data simply doesn't exist in most Indian bank systems — building it is a multi-year exercise
  • Massive skills gap — risk teams have no climate expertise, sustainability teams have no credit risk background
  • Scenario analysis methodology is technically complex and expensive — most Indian banks have never done this
Practitioner's Take

This framework will create more sustainability jobs in Indian financial services than any other regulation over the next three years. The professionals who understand both climate and finance will be exceptionally difficult to replace. This is the highest-value skill adjacency in Indian ESG right now.

Official Sources RBI Draft Framework (Feb 2024) RBI Discussion Paper on Climate Risk (Jul 2022)
03
Carbon Markets ◑ Phased Rollout
Carbon Credit Trading Scheme (CCTS)
Ministry of Power / BEE · Energy-intensive industries · Trading from FY2026
+
Applies To
Steel, cement, aluminium, fertilisers, textiles, chemicals, pulp & paper
Key Deadline
461 obligated entities notified by BEE across 9 sectors. GHG emission intensity targets being set for compliance year starting April 2025. Compliance trades expected October 2026.
Data Readiness Gap
High
So What

India's domestic carbon market is now operational in its foundational phase. The Carbon Credit Trading Scheme puts a price on carbon for 9 energy-intensive sectors — converting emissions reduction from a voluntary commitment to a financial instrument with real rupee value. With 461 obligated entities notified and compliance trades expected from October 2026, this is no longer future policy. It is active compliance infrastructure.

Where Companies Struggle
  • Facility-level GHG accounting is new for most — corporate totals exist but plant-level verified data does not
  • Carbon price uncertainty makes abatement investment decisions difficult
  • Verification ecosystem is nascent — accredited verifiers with industrial Scope 1 expertise are scarce
Latest update: The Ministry of Power has indicated the first compliance-based carbon credit trades are expected by October 2026. As of early 2026, 461 obligated entities across 9 sectors have been notified by BEE. The voluntary offset mechanism is already operational.
Practitioner's Take

CCTS is the most significant structural shift in Indian climate policy since PAT. Sustainability professionals who understand carbon accounting, verification standards, and trading mechanics will be in a completely different boardroom conversation than those who only know reporting frameworks.

Official Sources BEE Carbon Market Official Page PIB — Indian Carbon Market Update

ESG Ratings ● Effective
SEBI ESG Rating Providers (ERP) Framework
SEBI · All listed companies · Operational from 2024
+
Applies To
ESG Rating Providers; indirectly all listed companies receiving ratings
Key Deadline
All ERPs must be SEBI registered to operate; methodologies publicly disclosed
Data Readiness Gap
Medium
So What

ESG rating agencies are now under regulatory oversight for the first time — requiring methodology transparency and conflict-of-interest management. This elevates the credibility of ESG scores for investors and raises the stakes for companies on how they manage their ratings relationships.

Where Companies Struggle
  • Methodology differences between international ERPs and India-focused providers create inconsistent scores for the same company
  • Most companies still don't know which ERPs are assessing them or what data is being used
  • Reactive rather than proactive engagement — companies respond to poor scores rather than managing them
Practitioner's Take

The real gap is that Indian companies are being rated by global methodologies designed for western disclosure environments. A practitioner who can translate between global rating expectations and Indian disclosure reality — across CSA, MSCI, Sustainalytics simultaneously — is in a category of one in most organisations.

Official Sources SEBI Master Circular for ERPs (Jul 2023) SEBI FAQs on ERPs

Circular Economy ● Effective
Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) — Plastics, E-Waste & Batteries
MoEFCC · Producers, importers, brand owners · Mandatory from FY2022–23
+
Applies To
Producers, importers, brand owners of plastic packaging, electronics, batteries
Key Deadline
Annual EPR targets on CPCB portal; 100% collection target for plastics by FY2024–25
Data Readiness Gap
High
So What

EPR makes producers financially and operationally responsible for end-of-life management of their products and packaging. For FMCG companies this is no longer a voluntary sustainability commitment — it is a legal obligation with financial penalties. It also creates the foundation for India's circular economy transition.

Where Companies Struggle
  • Collection infrastructure is fragmented and informal — EPR compliance requires formalising relationships with the informal waste sector
  • EPR certificate markets are immature — prices volatile and verification of actual recycling inconsistent
  • Packaging redesign requires cross-functional commitment that most companies haven't built
Practitioner's Take

EPR is where sustainability meets operational reality most directly. Companies treating it as a checkbox are accumulating certificate shortfalls that will become expensive. The packaging sustainability skillset — EPR compliance, LCA thinking, supply chain redesign — is undervalued and growing fast in demand.

Official Sources CPCB EPR Portal MoEFCC Plastic Waste Management

Carbon Markets ◈ International ● Full Enforcement Active
EU Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (CBAM)
European Union · Indian steel, cement, aluminium, fertiliser exporters · Full financial obligations in effect from January 2026
+
Applies To
Indian exporters of steel, cement, aluminium, fertilisers, hydrogen to EU
Key Deadline
Quarterly emissions reporting now; CBAM certificates from Jan 2026
Data Readiness Gap
Critical
So What

CBAM is not a disclosure exercise — it is a tax. Indian companies exporting covered products to the EU pay a levy based on the carbon intensity of their production. For Indian steel exporters alone this could run into hundreds of crores annually if carbon intensity is not reduced. This is the most direct financial consequence of climate regulation for Indian exporters.

Where Companies Struggle
  • Product-level carbon accounting is fundamentally different from corporate reporting — no systems exist for this in most companies
  • EU prescribed methodology differs from what Indian companies currently track
  • SME exporters face the same legal obligations as large players but lack the resources to comply
Omnibus Update (2025): The EU Omnibus simplification package has exempted 90% of importers by volume from CBAM reporting scope. However, 99% of emissions remain in scope — meaning large Indian exporters in steel, cement, and aluminium are fully unaffected by this exemption. Full financial obligations remain from January 2026.
Practitioner's Take

CBAM is the easiest sustainability topic to get CFO attention on because it directly hits revenue. Practitioners who can explain it in financial terms — rupee impact per tonne, break-even carbon price, cost of delay — will find a far more receptive boardroom than those still talking in ESG score points.

Official Sources EU CBAM Official Page EU-India CBAM Cooperation Note

Disclosure & Reporting ◈ International ◑ Phased
EU Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (CSRD)
European Union · Indian subsidiaries of EU companies · FY2024 onwards phased
+
Applies To
Indian subsidiaries of EU companies; Indian cos listed on EU markets; large Indian cos with significant EU revenue
Key Deadline
Large EU entities: FY2024; others phased to FY2028. Assured reporting from year one.
Data Readiness Gap
Critical
So What

CSRD is the most comprehensive sustainability reporting requirement ever enacted. For Indian MNC subsidiaries this means European Sustainability Reporting Standards — dramatically more demanding than BRSR. Double materiality, full value chain disclosure, assured reporting. Indian sustainability teams in scope companies face significant workload expansion.

Where Companies Struggle
  • Double materiality is conceptually new — most Indian teams are accustomed to single financial materiality
  • ESRS requirements are far more granular than BRSR — the data gap is substantial
  • Assurance requirements are more demanding than anything currently required in India
Omnibus / Stop-the-Clock Update (April 2025): The EU Parliament adopted the Stop-the-Clock Directive delaying CSRD obligations. Wave 2 companies now report in 2028 (for FY2027), Wave 3 in 2029 (for FY2028). Wave 1 large listed companies (500+ employees) continue as planned — reporting FY2024 data. Indian subsidiaries of large EU companies remain in scope on the original timeline.
Practitioner's Take

CSRD will divide Indian sustainability professionals into two groups — those who understand it and those who don't. It is also the framework that will shape the next iteration of BRSR. Investing in ESRS and double materiality knowledge now is investing in the future direction of all Indian disclosure requirements.

Official Sources EU CSRD Official Page CSRD Directive Full Text

Sustainable Finance ● Effective
SEBI Green Bond Framework & Sustainable Finance Disclosure
SEBI / IFSCA · Green bond issuers, ESG mutual funds · 2023
+
Applies To
Listed companies issuing green/sustainable bonds; mutual funds with ESG mandates
Key Deadline
Pre and post-issuance disclosure; annual ESG mutual fund portfolio disclosure
Data Readiness Gap
High
So What

India's sustainable finance market is growing rapidly — green bond issuances crossed $10 billion in 2023. SEBI's framework brings regulatory credibility by defining eligible green categories, requiring use-of-proceeds tracking, and mandating impact reporting. This creates a direct link between sustainability performance and capital market access.

Where Companies Struggle
  • Impact quantification methodology is inconsistent — making genuine comparison across issuers impossible
  • Gap between use-of-proceeds tracking and actual environmental additionality is significant
  • SEBI framework differs from ICMA and CBI in places — creating complexity for companies targeting international investors
Practitioner's Take

Sustainable finance is where the money is — literally. As India scales its green bond market, practitioners who can structure use-of-proceeds frameworks and quantify environmental impact will be highly valued in treasury, investor relations, and sustainability functions.

Official Sources SEBI Green Debt Securities Circular IFSCA ESG Securities Framework

Climate Policy ● Effective
National Action Plan on Climate Change (NAPCC) & India's NDC
MoEFCC / Multiple Ministries · Broad corporate relevance · Net Zero 2070
+
Key Targets
45% emissions intensity reduction by 2030 vs 2005; 50% non-fossil power by 2030; Net zero 2070
Corporate Relevance
Sets direction for all downstream regulations. Shapes investor expectations.
Data Readiness Gap
Medium
So What

India's national climate commitments set the policy direction all downstream regulations flow from. Understanding the 8 NAPCC missions helps sustainability professionals anticipate where regulatory pressure is heading next — and position their companies' strategies ahead of mandates rather than behind them.

Practitioner's Take

Companies that build sustainability strategy around India's long-term climate trajectory — rather than just current mandatory requirements — will be ahead when the next regulation wave arrives. The 2070 net zero commitment will generate significant regulatory activity between now and then. Understanding this architecture separates strategic practitioners from reporting technicians.

Official Sources India Updated NDC (2022) MoEFCC — NAPCC India Long-Term Low Carbon Strategy

Supply Chain & Human Rights ◈ International ◑ Phased
EU Corporate Sustainability Due Diligence Directive (CSDDD)
European Union · Indian suppliers to EU companies · Indirect pressure now, direct from 2027
+
Applies To
Indian suppliers to EU companies; Indian cos with significant EU operations
Key Deadline
First phase application: 2029. Despite regulatory delays, Indian suppliers are already receiving due diligence questionnaires from EU customers now — buyer-driven pressure precedes legal mandates.
Data Readiness Gap
Critical
So What

CSDDD requires EU companies to conduct due diligence on human rights and environmental impacts across their entire value chain — including Indian suppliers. Even though Indian companies aren't directly regulated, they will face intense scrutiny from EU customers. Companies that cannot demonstrate robust practices risk losing EU business.

Where Companies Struggle
  • Indian suppliers lack documentation and systems to respond credibly to CSDDD-level due diligence
  • Human rights due diligence is genuinely new for most Indian corporate sustainability teams
  • Supply chain mapping beyond Tier 1 is operationally demanding and data-poor in most Indian supply chains
Omnibus Update (2025): The Stop-the-Clock Directive delayed CSDDD transposition by one year — member states now have until July 2027. First phase application is delayed to 2029 (largest companies: 3,000+ employees, €9B+ turnover). Indian suppliers will still receive due diligence questionnaires from EU customers from 2025–26 onwards regardless of these delays.
Practitioner's Take

CSDDD will hit Indian export-oriented industries harder than any other international regulation over the next three years — covering both environmental and human rights dimensions. Textiles, automotive components, pharma, food processing are all in scope through their EU customer relationships. Practitioners who understand human rights due diligence methodology will find significant demand in currently underprepared Indian companies.
